When I entered into my freshman year, I had never written a line of code, analyzed a circuit, or developed any hardware projects. I only enrolled in computer engineering because I loved building computers. It was an outlet for me to trouble shoot and problem solve; figuring out which parts were compatible with which and why that was. It made me challenge myself, as it taught me that satisfaction is reaped through hard work.

Now, entering into my third year, I look forward to programming almost everyday. I've found a love for problem-solving far beyond my initial intentions. What helped me make this discovery was my COM S 309 class where I got to develop an android application. This process was so much fun that I would wake up everyday and get excited to work on a new feature, table, or relationship. There were so many things that I had to learn in order to be successful. And that challenge was what made me fall in love with programming. That ability to grow continuously and always learn new ways to approach a problem, never ceased to amaze me.

But my whole personality doesn't solely revolve around my academics and professional career. I absolutely love the outdoors. Hiking, Fishing, Climbing, Biking. Pretty much any activity that's outside, I have fallen in love with. Climbing especially, this is because I used to be deathly afraid of heights. So, I joined a climbing club in high school where I slowly overcame that fear by constantly putting myself in uncomfortable situations, forcing myself to grow.

As I prepare to enter my third year of computer engineering, I reflect on my journey from a novice who had never written a line of code to a passionate programmer. My initial fascination with building computers has blossomed into a deep love for problem-solving and software development. The transformative experience of creating an Android application in my COM S 309 class ignited this passion, teaching me the joy of tackling challenges and continuously learning. Outside of academics, my love for the outdoors, especially climbing, has taught me resilience and the thrill of overcoming fears. This balance between my academic pursuits and outdoor adventures shapes who I am, driving me to embrace growth and new experiences in all aspects of life.
